Ivy is a third year at UCLA studying Film Production (Directing). She believes that stories can be told through any medium and is working towards creating interactive stories that combine her strengths: leadership, storytelling, and visual arts.


At 8, she studied at a boarding school in Shanghai for 4 years. Because of this, Ivy is ﬂuent in Mandarin, English, and proﬁcient in Japanese. Throughout middle school and high school, she won numerous awards for her artwork, including Federal Junior Duck Stamp (2nd place), Congressional Art Competition (2nd place), and California Coastal Art Commission (work is on the cover of state amendment papers). She holds many leadership roles and is praised for her project management and team building skills. Ivy studied at UC Davis for two years (B.A. Design minoring in Computer Science) and decided to shoot a ﬁlm on a whim. She unexpectedly found her love for ﬁlmmaking and storytelling, though her design and CS background have assisted her creative endeavors in numerous ways. Additionally, Ivy has studied directing and storytelling under Francis Glebas, the writer of Lion King, Pocahantos, Aladdin, Hercules, etc.


Ivy is recognized not only at UCLA, the Asian-American community, but also online. She has thousands of followers in art communities and has been published on the Daily Bruin and the World Journal (the largest Chinese newspaper). She has been interviewed for her work as a ﬁlm director and her work has been reviewed and published by multiple news outlets.


Besides watching and making movies, Ivy likes to go out and explore the nature and city. She enjoys drawing, reading a good novel under warm daylight, and taking long naps. She hopes to one day revolutionize our cinematic experience, give voice to underrepresented individuals, and change the world for the better
